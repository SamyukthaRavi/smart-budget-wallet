# User Experience Flow

This document describes how users interact with the Smart Budget Wallet feature inside a digital payment application.

---

## Step 1: Income Received

At the beginning of the month, the user receives income such as salary or allowance.

Example:

Salary credited: ₹60,000

The user opens the budgeting feature to allocate funds across categories.

---

## Step 2: Budget Allocation

The user creates envelope budgets for different categories.

Example allocation:

Rent → ₹15,000  
Food → ₹8,000  
Travel → ₹3,000  
Shopping → ₹4,000  
Entertainment → ₹2,000  
Savings → ₹10,000  

The system stores these allocations for the current month.

---

## Step 3: User Makes a Payment

The user makes a payment using the digital payment application.

Example:

Swiggy order → ₹500

---

## Step 4: Transaction Categorization

The system automatically categorizes the transaction.

Example:

Merchant: Swiggy  
Category: Food

---

## Step 5: Budget Deduction

The amount is deducted from the corresponding envelope.

Example:

Food Budget Before: ₹8,000  
Transaction: ₹500  
Food Budget After: ₹7,500

---

## Step 6: Budget Monitoring

Users can view:

- Remaining balance in each category
- Percentage of budget used
- Monthly spending patterns

---

## Step 7: Budget Alerts

The system notifies users when budgets approach limits.

Examples:

- 80% of Food budget used
- Shopping budget exhausted

---

## Step 8: Monthly Reset

At the beginning of a new month:

- Budgets reset
- Remaining funds may optionally move to savings
